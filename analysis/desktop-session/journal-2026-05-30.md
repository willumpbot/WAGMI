## Finding 1 — Skip quality is high on most symbols, ETH is the exception

**Methodology:** For each of 131 counterfactual skips, walk forward through 5m candles and classify by outcome (TP1 hit / SL hit / max favorable vs adverse excursion).

**Results (43 of 131 had enough subsequent price action to resolve):**

| Symbol | Skips evaluated | "Good skips" (price went against trade) | "Missed wins" (price went in our favor) | Skip quality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| HYPE | 29 | 29 | 0 | 100% |
| SOL | 8 | 8 | 0 | 100% |
| ETH | 6 | 0 | 6 | **0%** |
| BTC | 0 | — | — | n/a |

**Trader's read:** On HYPE and SOL, the LLM's caution is well-calibrated — every skipped trade would have lost. On ETH, every single skip would have won. That's not random. The bot has a calibration drift specifically on ETH.

**Caveat:** Small sample (6 ETH skips), measurement window is short (most signals haven't had enough time to fully play out at TP1/SL targets that take many hours). Six identical outcomes IS unusual though — coin-flip probability is ~1.5%.

## Finding 2 — Graduated-rule outcome tracking is broken

**What I checked:** `bot/data/llm/graduated_rules.json` — 10 rules the bot has learned.

**The problem:** Every single rule shows `times_correct: 0` despite some firing hundreds of times:

| Rule | Action | times_applied | times_correct | times_correct_rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| night_session_block_v1 | veto | 178 | 0 | broken |
| sol_long_veto_v1 | veto | 66 | 0 | broken |
| conf_floor_70_v1 | penalize | 47 | 0 | broken |
| hype_long_veto_v1 | veto | 43 | 0 | broken |
| btc_trend_boost (rule 1) | boost | 3 | 0 | broken |
| illiquid_regime_penalize_v1 | penalize | 1 | 0 | broken |
| eth_trending_regime_boost_v1 | boost | **0** | 0 | never fires |

**Trader's read:** The bot has "learned rules" but the feedback loop that validates whether they were right is broken. The bot is firing these rules with zero validation. Worse, every rule is in this state — not a sampling issue, a systemic broken-feedback-loop.

## Finding 3 — Regime-label mismatch: ETH boost rule never fires

**The rule** (`eth_trending_regime_boost_v1`):
```json
"conditions": {"symbol": "ETH", "regime": "trend"},
"action": "boost", "adjustment": 8.0,
"hypothesis": "ETH in TRENDING regime = 71% WR, +$9.49/trade EV on 7 live trades."
"times_applied": 0
```

**Why it never fires:** The condition checks for `regime: 'trend'`. But the live bot's regime detector reports ETH as `'trending_bull'` when ADX is high (saw this in the bot_thinking dashboard live). The labels don't match.

**Trader's read:** We have a rule that says "boost ETH when trending" — exactly what should fire on the trades the bot just missed. It never fires because of a string-label mismatch. The hurtful penalize/veto rules don't have this issue (they match on symbol+side, not regime).

**Bias direction:** The rule system is asymmetric — the harmful rules fire constantly, the helpful boost rule for the symbol we're missing wins on (ETH) never fires.

## Finding 4 — Most ETH skips happened in a window before LLM_FIRST_MODE was live

**Context:** I patched in `LLM_FIRST_MODE=true` to convert graduated-rule vetoes from hard-blocks to informational sometime around 19:55 UTC today. The 6 ETH missed-win counterfactuals were logged at 18:28-18:32 UTC — BEFORE that patch was live.

**What this means:** The bot was hard-vetoing those ETH trades, not just consulting the LLM about them. Post-patch, the same rule would still note the veto-source but the LLM would decide whether to honor it.

**Trader's read:** Some of the historical "skip quality" picture reflects an old, more rigid bot. The new LLM-as-decider mode hasn't been live long enough to have its own counterfactual track record. We need more time, OR we need a stricter ETH check.

## Finding 5 — Sample-size honesty

Before celebrating or panicking:

- 131 counterfactuals is a small sample
- 43 resolved is smaller
- 6 ETH = single-digit sample
- 3 hours of measurement window = too short for the bot's typical hold time
- Methodology uses arbitrary MFE/MAE thresholds (1.5x, 0.3%) — different cutoffs give different counts

**What's robust:** The graduated_rules.json content (rule definitions, times_applied counts) — those are observed facts from the data file.

**What's directional:** Skip quality patterns by symbol — interesting signal, but n=6 on ETH is one decent crypto pump away from being noise.

**What's speculation:** Specific cause-and-effect ("the regime label mismatch is why ETH boost never fires") — well-supported but I haven't traced the actual code path that evaluates the rule.
